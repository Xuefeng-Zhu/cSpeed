About cSpeed
cSpeed tests the quality of your browsing experience by directly measuring how much time it takes to load a list of popular Webpages. cSpeed does not collect any personal Web-browsing information. We do record your IP address, city, and browser user-agent (which includes your operating system and browser version). We also run a small computational test to measure javascript performance.

We (Xuefeng Zhu, Ankit Singla, P. Brighten Godfrey) are computer scientists at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, working on making the Internet faster. The first step, of course, is to measure how fast the Internet is today. We developed cSpeed with twin objectives: (a) to measure Internet speed across a diverse set of users; and (b) to provide you a way of comparing Internet service options in your area. The bandwidth numbers (Mbps / Gbps) reported by ISPs and other 'speed tests' are not as meaningful as the actual performance we measure.
